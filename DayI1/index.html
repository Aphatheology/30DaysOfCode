Umaru Musa Yar'Adua

The 13th president of the Federal Republic of Nigeria

(16 August 1951 – 5 May 2010)

  • 1951 - Umaru Yar'Adua was born in Katsina State, Nigeria
  • 1958 - He started his primary education at Rafukka Primary School, Katsina
  • 1962 - He left Rafukka for Dutsinma Boarding Primary School from where he completed his primary education in 1964
  • 1965-1969 - Umaru Yar’adua was at Government College, Keffi in present-day Nasarawa State for his secondary education
  • 1970-1971 - He moved to the famous Barewa College Zaria for his Higher School Certificate between
  • 1972-1975 - He attended the Ahmadu Bello University (ABU) Zaria where he obtained B.Sc in Education/Chemistry
  • 1975-1976 - His working career began at the Holy Child College, Lagos for the mandatory one year National Youth Service Corps (NYSC)
  • 1976-1979 - He was a Lecturer at the Katsina College of Arts, Science and Technology, Zaria. He moved to Katsina Polytechnic, also as a Lecturer in 1979
  • 1978-1980 - He returned to the the Ahmadu Bello University (ABU) Zaria for his M.Sc Degree in Analytical Chemistry
  • 1978-1983 - He served as Member Governing Council of both Katsina College of Arts, Science and Technology Zaria and Katsina Polytechnic
  • 1979-1983 - During the Second Republic, Yar' Adua was a member of the leftist People's Redemption Party, while his father was briefly the National Vice chairman of the National Party of Nigeria
  • 1983 - He left the public service
  • 1983-1989 - His movement to the private sector started at Sambo Farms Ltd in Funtua, Katsina State as its pioneer General Manager
  • 1984-1985 - He served as a Board Member, Katsina State Farmers’ Supply Company
  • He served as a Director of many companies, including :
  • 1983-1999 - Hamada Holdings
  • 1995-1999 - Habib Nigeria Bank Ltd
  • 1987-1999 - Lodigiani Nigeria Ltd
  • 1987-1999 - Madara Ltd. Vom, Jos
  • 1994-1996 - Board Chairman of Katsina State Investment and Property Development Company (KIPDECO)
  • 1995-1999 - He was Chairman, Nation House Press Ltd, Kaduna
  • 1991 - He contested the 1991 Governorship election, but lost to Saidu Barda, the candidate of the National Republican Convention and an ally of President Ibrahim Babangida.
  • 1999- He ran for the same position and won
  • 2000 - During his administration as governor, Katsina became the fifth northern Nigerian state to adopt sharia
  • 2003 - He was re-elected. He also became the first governor to publicly declare his assets
  • 2006 - Yar'Adua was chosen as the presidential candidate of the ruling PDP for the April 2007 election
  • 2007 - he was flown to Germany for medical reasons
  • 2007 - In the presidential election, Yar'Adua won with 70% of the vote (24.6 million votes), according to official results released on April 23
  • 2007 - After the election, Yar'Adua proposed a government of national unity.
  • 2007 - In late June 2007, two opposition parties, the All Nigeria Peoples Party (ANPP) and the Progressive Peoples Alliance (PPA), agreed to join Yar'Adua's government.
  • 2007 - Yar'Adua publicly revealed his declaration of assets from May (becoming the first Nigerian Leader to do so), according to which he had 856,452,892 naira ($5 million) in assets, 19 million naira($ 0.1 million) of which belonged to his wife. He also had 88,793,269.77 naira ($0.5 million) in liabilities. This disclosure, which fulfilled a pre-election promise he made, was intended to set an example for other Nigerian politicians and discourage corruption.
  • 2007 - Yar'Adua's new cabinet was sworn in. It included 39 ministers, including two from the ANPP
  • 2009 - President Yar'Adua left Nigeria on 23 November 2009, and was reported to be receiving treatment for pericarditis at a clinic in Saudi Arabia. He was not seen in public again and his absence created a power vacuum in Nigeria
  • 2010 - On February 9, 2010, the Senate determined that presidential power should be transmitted to the Vice President Goodluck Jonathan. He was to serve as Acting President, with all the accompanied powers, until Yar'Adua returned to full health.
  • 2010 - February 24, 2010, Yar'Adua returned to Abuja. His state of health was unclear, but there was speculation that he was still on a life support machine
  • 2010 - Yar'Adua died on May 5, 2010, 9 P.M. local time at the Aso Rock presidential villa.
